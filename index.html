Sura Hawaii Korean BBQ

Formally known as 678 Hawaii, this Oahu staple has just recently broke off from the popular 678/ Baekjong franchise in 2016. In keeping with the Korean tradition of slow cooking, they stress the importance of your body's well-being. That's why they use only prime quality meats and other fresh ingredients in their dishes. As the old saying goes, variety is the spice of life, and you'll find plenty of variety at this family-friendly eatery. From traditional Korean barbecue dishes including Bibimbap and kimchi pancakes to contemporary creations like spicy pork ribs, Korean style box lunches, and beef galbi-jjim, Sura Hawaii's lunch and dinner menus are packed with flavor. Every table has its very own built-in stovetop, allowing guests to look on as a member of the wait staff cooks their meal.

Iamanas Tei Restaurant

Tucked away near the University of Hawaii right on King street, Imanas Tei is a tradition Japanese seafood restaurant in a cozy bamboo-accented space. Imanas Tei has uses simple and fresh ingredients excecuted by the skilled sushi chefs working at the bar.

Helena's Hawaiian Food

Helena's is known by both locals and tourists alike to be home of some of the best authentic Hawaiian food such their Kalua pork & cabbage, lomi lomi salmon, and their ever so popular Pipikaula.
